Women Helpline Number (181): 24x7 emergency helpline for women in distress. Provides immediate assistance, legal guidance, and medical aid. Ensures rescue and rehabilitation services.
Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP): Awareness campaign to improve the declining child sex ratio (CSR). Focuses on gender equality, female education, and reducing discrimination. Encourages community participation to bring social change.
Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandhana Yojna (PMMVY): Financial assistance of ₹5,000 for pregnant and lactating mothers (first living child). Paid in three installments based on health checkups and vaccinations. Aims to reduce maternal mortality and encourage institutional deliveries.
One Stop Centre (Sakhi Centre): Provides legal, medical, psychological, and temporary shelter support to women facing violence.
State Resource Centre for Women (SRCW): Ensures implementation of women-related laws and policies. Provides gender training for state officials.
Working Women Hostel: Safe and affordable accommodation for working women. Includes daycare facilities for children.
Kishori Shakti Yojna: Targets adolescent girls (11-18 years old). Provides health check-ups, self-defense training, and skill development.
Compensation Scheme for Women Victims/Survivors of Sexual Assault & Other Crimes (2018): Financial compensation for victims of: Rape & sexual assault (₹4-10 lakh). Trafficking & other crimes (₹3-7 lakh). Acid attacks (₹7-12 lakh).
Scheme for Relief and Rehabilitation of Women Acid Attack Victims: Free medical treatment & reconstructive surgeries. Financial aid up to ₹10 lakh. Legal assistance & counseling.
Protection Against Domestic Violence: Provides legal aid, shelter, and counseling to victims of domestic violence.
Mukhya Mantri Matritav Sahayta Yojana: ₹5,000 maternity benefit for the second child (boy).
State Level Award for Women: Recognizes women achievers in education, entrepreneurship, social work, and more.
Balika Samriddhi Yojana: launched on 2nd October 1997 with the objective of raising the overall status of the girl-child and bringing about a positive change in family and community attitudes. The scheme covers up two girls children born on or after 15th August 1997 in a family living below poverty line as defined by the Government of India.
Women Helpline (WHL): is intended to provide 24 hours assistance and emergency response to women affected by violence. The helpline also provides information about women related government schemes programs across the country through a single uniform number.
